I Self-Created a LinkedIn Job to See Which Resume Format Passes ATS

Originally posted by SpecialistArm7522 on Reddit

Over the past few weeks, I was experimenting with LinkedIn by creating several accounts with different setups to see which resume formats were more likely to pass LinkedIn filters and reach a hiring manager.

Out of 56 candidates, only 18 appeared directly in my inbox. For the others, I had to manually look in the "Not a Fit" (spam) folder to even find their applications. These candidates receive a rejection letter three days later, likely to not discourage them immediately. But effectively, they're already rejected.

I want to be transparent: I’m sharing a LaTeX formatted resume for those who want the best format, and also a drag-and-drop UI tool for converting resumes to PDF using LaTeX. Both options are open source and free. No signup is needed.

Key LinkedIn Resume Filters Discovered

  • The most brutal filter is if your country is different from the one where the job is located.
  • If the job is Hybrid or On-Site, and your location is too far (even within the same country), there's a high chance of being auto-rejected.
  • Using a foreign phone number (wrong country code) is another common rejection reason.
  • Resume formatting matters. Some fancy PDF templates or Word files can confuse LinkedIn’s parser. Use simple, clean formatting.
  • Avoid keyword stuffing — comma-separated or bullet lists of tech stacks at the bottom no longer help and can trigger spam filters. Instead, naturally embed keywords in your job descriptions. Use bold text for emphasis when needed.

Disclaimer: This experiment was conducted for research purposes only. The results may vary across different industries or over time.
